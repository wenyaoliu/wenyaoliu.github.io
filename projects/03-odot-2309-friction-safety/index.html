3. Utilizing Pavement Friction Data for the Reduction of Traffic Crashes

2021-11-26
1 min read
Statistics
Transportation

Sponsored by the Oklahoma Department of Transportation (ODOT), this study identifies the contributing factors to traffic crashes and provides decision-making models.

Major Works

  • Used SQL and R to clean, unify and filter data from four state-maintained data sets.
  • Analyzed with R on friction demands and influencing factors of crashes using various statistical models: negative binomial (NB), hurdle NB and zero-inflated NB regression. Model performances were evaluated with AIC, BIC, and residual plots.
  • Developed VBA-based spreadsheet software integrating the proposed model and safety costs in life cycle cost analysis (LCCA).

Outcomes

Published 2 peer-reviewed papers, developed 1 software, and drafted the final report passing the requirements of Oklahoma DOT at one submission.

Yu. 2022. “Integrating Skid Resistance and Safety Benefits into Life Cycle Cost Analysis for Pavement Surface Treatment Selection.” Journal of Transportation Engineering, Part B: Pavements, 148 (2): 04022015.
